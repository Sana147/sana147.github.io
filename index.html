"Research is formalized curiosity. It is poking and prying with a purpose."               Zora Neale Hurston

I am a PhD student, under the awe-inspiring supervision of Dr. Jedidiah R. Crandall in the Biodesign Center for Biocomputation, Security, and Society.

My research focuses on identifying and fixing vulnerabilities in Android apps that come from the app-vendor and the network infrastructure itself. The goal of my PhD is to outline these vulnerabitlies and work with app-vendors towards fixing them to imrpove Internet freedom for at-risk populations under authoritarian regimes.

Before joining Dr. Crandall’s research group, my research was focused on corporate-centered threats. I spent a little over three years under the awesome mentorship of Dr. Adam Doupé, where I worked on developing and building frameworks for improving security for Software-Defined Networks (SDN).
